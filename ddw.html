About

In this second-year information design class, we worked on a 5-week project to create a captivating microsite for Dutch Design Week, an annual design conference. The initial three weeks were dedicated to visual experimentation, where we explored design qualities and principles to help guide our decisions. During this phase, our exploration aimed to create a flexible visual direction that could define the unique identity of the microsite.

02 Process

The first two weeks were spent exploring different visual identities. We were assigned designer, Massimo Vignelli and studied his work to surface qualities and principles that we could use help influence are graphic exploration. This exercise was a valuable learning experience in understanding why mockups are important. I thought they were to get a sense of what our posters would look like in a real-world context but, I soon found out they provide much more than that. As a result, these assets were rather weak.

03 Context Driven Assets

After receiving feedback from the teaching team and Learning from my previous iterations, I realized that the mockups should showcase a design inspired by the qualities of the poster, not just the poster itself. My new goal became to 1. create mockups that showcase the flexibility and versatility of the visual identity and 2. Use locations and landmarks within a geographical context to add cultural significance.
