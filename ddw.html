About

In this second-year information design class, we worked on a 5-week project to create a captivating microsite for Dutch Design Week, an annual design conference. The project aimed to establish an intuitive and comprehensive platform that guided visitors through the diverse offerings of the festival which hosts over 100 events with a global audience. In the creation of the Dutch Design Week microsite, I played a pivotal role in shaping the interaction design, developing mockups, incorporating motion design, and contributing to research.

Content Design

Our goal was to empower visitors with a strategic planning tool. Utilizing content design, we provide detailed insights into schedules, locations, and unique aspects of each exhibition. This approach allows attendees to optimize their schedules, making the most of their time at Dutch Design Week.

Navigation

The dropdown navigation helps declutter the homepage and offer a dedicated space for easy navigation. The animation of typography within the dropdown also adds dynamism, making the navigation experience more memorable.

Event Page

The event page served as a dynamic planning tool, organized by date and time, with alphabetical ordering for simultaneous events. Filter chips were introduced, allowing users to customize their exploration based on date, time, discipline, and location. .

Personalized Itinerary

Recognizing the vastness of Dutch Design Week, the itinerary aims to offer attendees a tool that not only optimizes their schedules but also fosters a sense of personalization and control over their festival experience..

Graphic Assets

When creating mockups, my focus shifted towards identifying locations and landmarks in the Netherlands that held cultural relevance, ensuring that every visual element communicated a connection to the rich tapestry of Dutch culture.
